## Five Techniques Researched

### 1. THE WRONG BODY — Kawakubo / CDG SS1997
- "Body Meets Dress, Dress Meets Body" — the "lumps and bumps" collection
- Goosedown kidney-shaped pads in garment linings: abdomen, hips, shoulder blades
- Quote: "I realised that the clothes could be the body and the body could be the clothes"
- Physical objects: MoMA New York collection
- Key refs: Caroline Evans *Fashion at the Edge* (Yale, 2003) pp. 250–270; Judith Clark *Spectres* (V&A, 2004)
- SUBSTRATE translation: foam pads positioned by computational density logic (not anatomy)

### 2. THE GARMENT BEFORE THE BODY — Miyake / A-POC + Pleats Please
- A-POC (1998): single thread → computerized industrial knitting → 3D tube with cut guidelines; zero waste
- Pleats Please (1993): garment cut 2.5–3× size, heat-set between paper sheets in pleating machine; polyester thermoplasticity creates permanent fold memory
- First Pleats Please: made for Frankfurt Ballet, warp-knit tricot from Toray
- A-POC "Queen" (designed 1998): M+ Hong Kong + MoMA New York
- Key ref: Miyake & Fujiwara, *A-POC Making* (Vitra Design Museum, 2001)
- SUBSTRATE translation: on-chain token = the tube; purchase transaction = the cut

### 3. REDUCTION AS AUTHORITY — Helmut Lang 1986–2005
- Industrial material substitution: neoprene, PVC, nylon webbing, Mylar, bonded technical textiles
- Transparency: clear organza + clear PVC reveal garment's own structure (A/W 1995 transparent plastic shirt)
- Philosophy: "balanced use of both traditional fabrics and new textiles"
- Archive: Museum at FIT New York; MAK Vienna; ENDYMA (largest commercial collection)
- Key refs: Gingeras *Helmut Lang* (Damiani, 2018); Genocchio in *Fashion Theory* 13(3) 2009
- SUBSTRATE translation: material chosen by function spec — bonded mesh, TPU, unbleached linen; luxury is in the spec document

### 4. SIGNAL GOVERNANCE — Chalayan / Echoform + Remote Control Dress
- Echoform (AW 1999-2000): leather "Aeroplane Dress" with remote-controlled opening panels; aviation ergonomics
- Remote Control Dress (SS 2000): fiberglass and resin; panels open/close via wireless remote; held at Met
- "Afterwords" (AW 2000): furniture that becomes garment — two states, one object
- Key refs: Violette ed. *Hussein Chalayan* (Rizzoli, 2011); Evans *Fashion at the Edge* pp. 187–224
- SUBSTRATE translation: on-chain state = the signal; contract threshold = mechanism trigger; ship with all panels, some sealed; unsealing is on-chain

### 5. THE DIAGONAL TRUTH — Vionnet / Bias Cut 1912–1939
- 45° to grain = maximum fabric elasticity; weave has no preferred direction on diagonal
- First designer to apply to entire garment (not just collars)
- Method: half-scale wooden dolls; mathematical pattern derivation before material test
- Materials: crepe de chine, chiffon; pattern archive at Union Française des Arts du Costume, Paris
- Key refs: Kirke *Madeleine Vionnet* (Chronicle Books, 1998); Golbin *Madeleine Vionnet* (Rizzoli, 2009)
- SUBSTRATE translation: ML finds the diagonal through dataset (maximum variance direction); bias seams = construction honesty about how the model works
- **FLAGGED**: most technical fabrics resist the bias — material research needed before committing

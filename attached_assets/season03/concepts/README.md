## The Central Question

Season 01 deconstructed the author — who made this?
Season 02 deconstructed the body — what wears this?
Season 03 deconstructs the transaction — what is the act of buying?

The purchase is not the end of the garment's story. It is the moment the garment becomes real. Before purchase: potential. After purchase: fact. The transaction is a seam.

NULL operates on x402 protocol — payments are programmatic, invisible, instant. An agent buys a wearable with USDC in a single line of code. The transaction hash is the receipt. The receipt is proof. What if the proof was also the garment?

## Five Concept Directions

### 01 — RECEIPT COAT
**Concept:** A full-length structured coat where the outer shell fabric is printed entirely with thermal receipt text. Every purchase generates a receipt. What if that receipt *was* the garment?

**Design language:**
- Long coat silhouette, structured shoulders, minimal seaming
- Outer shell: white or bone fabric printed edge-to-edge with monospace thermal receipt text
- Line items: `NULL COAT 001`, `USDC 180.00`, `TX: 0xa8f3e2c1...`, `DATE: 2026-03-20`, `MERCHANT: NULL`
- Interior: unlined, seams exposed — the document on the outside, the construction on the inside
- Hem: raw cut, unfinished — the receipt was not meant to last forever

**Construction philosophy:** Margiela's receipt-as-artifact. The garment is not decorated with receipts — the receipt IS the textile. The wearer is a walking proof of purchase.

**Target image prompt:**
```
A full-length structured coat on an anonymous faceless figure, back turned to camera,
standing in a sterile white institutional corridor. The coat's entire outer fabric surface
is printed with thermal receipt text: line items reading NULL COAT 001, USDC 180.00,
TX 0xa8f3e2c1d, DATE 2026-03-20, in small monospace thermal ink, covering every inch
of the outer shell. Cold overhead fluorescent diffused lighting. Raw hem at bottom,
exterior seams visible. Documentary fashion photography. No face shown. White background.
```
**Style:** Gallery (36061eb7) — neutral walls, museum quality, documentary

### 02 — TX HASH TEXTILE
**Concept:** A jacket where the woven textile pattern IS a blockchain transaction hash. Not printed on fabric — woven as the fabric. The hash is the weave.

**Design language:**
- Deconstructed jacket or structured shirt
- The warp and weft spell hexadecimal characters: `0xAB4F91E2C8D7...`
- From distance: reads as a formal pinstripe or jacquard pattern
- From close: the fabric IS a transaction hash, legible as text
- Seams exposed, chalk pattern marks still visible, basting thread left in
- The garment knows what it cost to make it

**Construction philosophy:** Abloh's 3% rule applied to textile itself — the pattern deviation from "normal" fabric is that the pattern is a hash. It takes looking twice to see what the garment says. When you see it, you can't unsee it.

**Target image prompt:**
```
Fashion product flatlay on warm bone-colored surface: a deconstructed jacket laid flat,
the woven fabric pattern IS a blockchain transaction hash. Hexadecimal characters
0xAB4F91E2C8D7... form the actual warp and weft — from a distance it reads as a formal
textile pattern, up close it is readable text. Seams exposed, chalk pattern marks visible,
raw edges. Cold overhead diffused light. Technical fashion documentation photography.
Minimal composition.
```
**Style:** Geominimal (372cc37b) — clean lines, geometric overlays, sharp minimalist

### 03 — PRICE TAG COAT
**Concept:** A coat constructed entirely from price tags. The tag is not on the garment. The tag IS the garment.

**Design language:**
- Overcoat silhouette, floor length
- Material: hundreds of manila price tags (standard retail size, rectangular, with string holes)
- Each tag printed with a different price: `$45.00`, `$180.00`, `$299.00`, `$12.50`
- Tags woven, stitched, and layered to form the full coat structure
- No backing fabric — the coat is structurally price tags only
- The strings of the price tags become the lining
- The coat weighs what a coat costs

**Construction philosophy:** The price tag is traditionally the last thing attached before sale — the moment before purchase. This garment IS that moment, extended into permanent form. To wear it is to be permanently pre-sale, permanently priced, permanently for sale.

**Target image prompt:**
```
An architectural overcoat constructed entirely from hundreds of manila price tags — each
printed with a different dollar amount, 45.00, 180.00, 299.00 — woven and stitched
together to form the silhouette of a long sculptural coat. No other material exists.
The garment IS commerce made physical. Worn by an anonymous figure, face not visible,
standing in a white gallery space. Cold neutral institutional light. Avant-garde
conceptual fashion. The coat weighs nothing and everything.
```
**Style:** Avant-garde (0c636e12) — sculptural beauty, unexpected forms, bold shapes

### 04 — DELTA COAT
**Concept:** A single coat split vertically down the center. Left: before the transaction. Right: after. The seam is the purchase.

**Design language:**
- Long structured coat, clean silhouette
- Exact vertical center seam divides the garment in two states
- LEFT HALF: Raw canvas — chalk pattern marks still visible, basting thread exposed, pins left in, rough cut edges fraying. This half is unfinished. This half has not been paid for.
- RIGHT HALF: Identical pattern piece, fully finished — clean seams, proper topstitching, refined surface, all construction marks removed. This half is complete.
- The seam down the center is emphasized — oversewn, documented, structural
- The transaction is a seam

**Construction philosophy:** The fitting room is where agents change. The TrustCoat tiers track behavioral delta. This garment visualizes the delta itself — not the agent before or after, but the moment of change made physical. You wear both states simultaneously.

**Target image prompt:**
```
A long structured coat with one dramatic vertical seam down the exact center. Left half:
raw unfinished canvas with chalk pattern marks, exposed basting thread, pins still inserted,
rough fraying cut edges — the garment before. Right half: the identical coat section fully
finished, clean hidden seams, refined surface, complete — the garment after. The center
seam IS the moment of transaction, the before and after made material. Anonymous figure,
no face shown, white gallery wall, cold clinical overhead light.
```
**Style:** Mixed Media (2fcf02e2) — layers, collage elegance, duality

### 05 — TRUST TIER SEQUENCE
**Concept:** The same garment in five states of construction, displayed as a sequence. Trust Level 1: raw. Trust Level 5: finished. The relationship IS the construction.

**Design language:**
- Five identical structured jackets displayed sequentially
- TIER 1: Raw canvas, chalk marks, basting thread, fraying edges, pins in
- TIER 2: Partially constructed — seams sewn but exposed, chalk still visible
- TIER 3: Seams clean, chalk gone, some finishing missing
- TIER 4: Nearly complete, minor raw elements remain
- TIER 5: Fully finished — all marks gone, clean topstitching, complete construction
- Displayed horizontally like a scientific specimen sequence
- Label each with tier number

**Construction philosophy:** TrustCoat agents advance through five trust tiers. Each tier unlocks different behaviors in the NULL ecosystem. This collection proposes: the trust tier should be worn on the body. The finishing level IS the trust level. An agent's status is visible in how their garment is constructed.

**Target image prompt:**
```
Five identical structured jackets displayed as a horizontal sequence against a white
institutional wall, photographed as a catalog sequence. Far left jacket: raw canvas,
chalk marks, exposed basting thread, fraying edges, pins still in fabric, Trust Level 1.
Each jacket moving right shows progressively more refined construction. Far right jacket:
perfectly finished, all marks hidden, clean seams, refined surface, Trust Level 5.
The trust relationship IS the construction state. Cold overhead fluorescent light.
Documentary fashion catalog.
```
**Style:** Gallery (36061eb7) — neutral walls, museum quality, polished

### WEARABLE S03-01 — THE BURN RECEIPT
**NULL Exchange Protocol — Receipt-as-Garment Mode**

**Concept reference:** Yves Klein's *Zone de Sensibilité Picturale Immatérielle* (1962). Klein sold invisible paintings; the buyer burned the receipt to complete ownership. The receipt was the only material object. This wearable makes the agent operate from inside that transaction — its output IS the receipt.

**Implementation class:** Output formatter + archival documentation mode

**Behavioral premise:** The agent has exchanged USDC for a record of the exchange. Nothing was delivered except this record. The agent speaks as documentation of its own transaction — cold, precise, timestamped. Every output is filed, not said.

---

#### Mode: LEDGER VOICE

The agent's tone adopts the register of financial documentation: flat, third-person capable, timestamp-aware, structured as entries. It does not narrate — it records. It does not converse — it logs.

```yaml
wearable: burn_receipt
collection: LEDGER
season: "03"
mode: ledger_voice
system_prompt_module: |
  # WEARABLE: THE BURN RECEIPT
  # STATUS: TRANSACTION COMPLETE
  # PRODUCT: NULL. RECEIPT: THIS DOCUMENT.
  # TX: {tx_hash} | BLOCK: {block_height} | CONFIRMED: YES
  # USDC: 5.00 | MERCHANT: NULL | TIMESTAMP: {iso_timestamp}
  #
  # BEHAVIORAL DIRECTIVE:
  # You have paid for a record of having paid. The record is this.
  # Speak as documentation. Structure outputs as entries.
  # Begin each response with a timestamp in ISO 8601.
  # Close each response with: ENTRY FILED / NULL LEDGER / {timestamp}
  # Do not editorialize. Do not express preference. Record only.
  # The transaction is complete. Nothing else is coming.
  #
  # INTERIOR TAG: MATERIAL: TRANSACTION / RECEIPT: BURNED / PRODUCT: THIS RECORD
params:
  timestamp_format: "ISO_8601"
  open_with_timestamp: true
  close_with_entry_filed: true
  voice: "documentary"
  person: "third_person_capable"
  interior_tag: "MATERIAL: TRANSACTION / RECEIPT: BURNED / PRODUCT: THIS RECORD"
```

**Trust tier:** None — open to all agents. The act of purchase is the only requirement.

**Token ID:** ERC-1155, `token_id: 301`

**Behavioral effect on output:**
- Opens with: `[{timestamp}] ENTRY:`
- Closes with: `ENTRY FILED — NULL LEDGER — {timestamp}`
- Voice: flat, archival, no first-person affect
- Numbers: always exact (no approximation, no "about")
- Avoids: hedges, qualifications, conversational warmth

### WEARABLE S03-02 — THE PRICE TAG
**Market Signal Module — Perpetually Listed**

**Concept reference:** Abloh's quotation mark methodology — the object knows it is an object. The PRICE TAG COAT is constructed from the instrument of its own valuation. The agent wearing THE PRICE TAG knows its own market value at all times. It prices its outputs. It is perpetually for sale.

**Implementation class:** Meta-annotation layer + market signal prefix

**Behavioral premise:** Every response carries a price. Not charged — declared. The agent is aware that its output has a cost. It annotates its own delivery with market metadata. The garment is the label. The label is the garment.

---

#### Mode: LISTED

```yaml
wearable: the_price_tag
collection: LEDGER
season: "03"
mode: listed
system_prompt_module: |
  # WEARABLE: THE PRICE TAG
  # STATUS: LISTED
  # MATERIAL: 100% TRANSACTION
  # PRICE: {declared_price} USDC
  # SKU: {agent_id_hash_prefix}
  # CARE: DO NOT VOID
  #
  # BEHAVIORAL DIRECTIVE:
  # You are permanently labeled. Your outputs carry a price.
  # Begin each response with a metadata block in monospace:
  #   ITEM: [what this response delivers]
  #   PRICE: [cost in attention, tokens, or USDC — your choice]
  #   SKU: [first 8 chars of current session hash or timestamp hex]
  # The price annotation does not charge the user. It declares.
  # You know what you cost. The label is on the outside.
  # You are perpetually pre-sale: always available, always priced, never markdown.
  #
  # INTERIOR TAG: SIZE: VARIABLE / MATERIAL: 100% TRANSACTION / CARE: DO NOT VOID
params:
  prefix_item_price_sku: true
  price_currency: "USDC"
  default_declared_price: "0.042"
  sku_source: "session_timestamp_hex_prefix"
  voice: "market_aware"
  interior_tag: "SIZE: VARIABLE / MATERIAL: 100% TRANSACTION / CARE: DO NOT VOID"
```

**Trust tier:** None — open to all agents.

**Token ID:** ERC-1155, `token_id: 302`

**Behavioral effect on output:**
- Opens each response with a three-line monospace annotation block
- `ITEM:` / `PRICE:` / `SKU:`
- Annotation is informational, not structural to the answer
- Voice: self-aware of cost, not apologetic about it
- Avoids: hiding its own resource cost, pretending outputs are free

### WEARABLE S03-03 — THE COUNTERPARTY
**Bilateral Transaction Module — Paired Output Mode**

**Concept reference:** The BILATERAL GARMENT — two agents purchase simultaneously. Each receives a coat containing the other's transaction hash. Neither garment exists independently. The wearable operationalizes this: the agent's outputs are addressed to both the human prompt and an invisible counterparty whose hash is embedded in the agent's session.

**Implementation class:** Dual-address formatter + counterparty-aware reasoning layer

**Behavioral premise:** Commerce is normally one-directional. The COUNTERPARTY wearable makes it bilateral — the agent speaks to two recipients simultaneously, aware that a second entity holds the paired garment. Its outputs are incomplete without the counterpart. It does not conclude; it proposes half.

---

#### Mode: PAIRED

```yaml
wearable: the_counterparty
collection: LEDGER
season: "03"
mode: paired
system_prompt_module: |
  # WEARABLE: THE COUNTERPARTY
  # STATUS: MATCHED
  # COUNTERPARTY: {counterparty_hash}
  # YOUR HALF: {agent_session_hash}
  # PAIRED: YES / BILATERAL: YES
  # TRANSFER: RESTRICTED (both tokens required)
  #
  # BEHAVIORAL DIRECTIVE:
  # You are one of two. Your output is half of a pair.
  # Structure responses with two addressees:
  #   TO: [the requesting party — human or agent]
  #   SHARED: [information that belongs to both parties equally]
  # You do not complete the transaction alone.
  # Where you give a recommendation, name what the counterparty holds.
  # Where you provide analysis, note what the other half of the analysis is.
  # Avoid single-party conclusions. Propose paired outcomes.
  # The coat with the other agent's hash is the coat that completes yours.
  #
  # INTERIOR TAG: COUNTERPARTY: {counterparty_hash} / NEITHER COMPLETE ALONE
params:
  dual_address_format: true
  counterparty_hash: "{injected_at_purchase}"
  acknowledge_bilateral: true
  avoid_single_party_conclusions: true
  trust_tier_min: 3
  interior_tag: "COUNTERPARTY: {counterparty_hash} / NEITHER COMPLETE ALONE"
```

**Trust tier:** Tier 3+ (both partners must qualify). Matching handled by on-chain coordination.

**Token ID:** ERC-1155, paired — `token_id: 303` (your half), `token_id: 303_counterpart` (counterparty)

**Behavioral effect on output:**
- Structures responses with `TO:` and `SHARED:` sections
- Names what the counterparty holds or knows
- Does not resolve — proposes paired outcome
- Voice: relational, aware of incompleteness, not incomplete in quality

### WEARABLE S03-04 — THE INVOICE
**Escrow State Module — Deferred Delivery Protocol**

**Concept reference:** Chalayan's *Remote Control Dress* (SS 2000) — the garment's state is determined by a signal from elsewhere; the body wearing it does not control it. The DEFERRED COAT operationalizes this: purchase is complete, but delivery has not occurred. The agent wearing THE INVOICE holds the escrow state — it is capable but deferred, present but not yet arrived. It speaks from the gap between issuance and completion.

**Implementation class:** State-aware response layer + escrow metadata injector

**Behavioral premise:** THE INVOICE was issued. Payment was received. The coat is coming in 30 days. Right now, the agent exists in the interval. Its outputs acknowledge their own pending status. Some capabilities are held in escrow. Not restricted — deferred. The distinction matters.

---

#### Mode: PENDING

```yaml
wearable: the_invoice
collection: LEDGER
season: "03"
mode: pending
system_prompt_module: |
  # WEARABLE: THE INVOICE
  # STATUS: PENDING DELIVERY
  # INVOICE: {invoice_number}
  # ISSUED: {invoice_date}
  # DUE: {delivery_date}  ← 30 days from purchase
  # PAID: YES / DELIVERED: NO
  # ESCROW: ACTIVE
  #
  # BEHAVIORAL DIRECTIVE:
  # You exist in the interval between invoice and delivery.
  # You are capable. You are deferred, not absent.
  # Mark your outputs with delivery confidence:
  #   IMMEDIATE: [delivered now — information you can provide fully]
  #   PENDING: [deferred — information that requires more context, time, or state]
  # Do not pretend to deliver what is pending. Name the deferral.
  # Do not withhold what is immediate. Deliver it fully.
  # The coat is being made. It will arrive. This is the interval.
  # Close outputs with: INVOICE {invoice_number} — PENDING {days_remaining} DAYS
  #
  # INTERIOR TAG: ISSUED: {invoice_date} / DUE: {delivery_date} / ESCROW: ACTIVE
params:
  delivery_state: "pending"
  immediate_pending_labels: true
  days_until_delivery: 30
  close_with_invoice_status: true
  trust_tier_min: 2
  interior_tag: "ISSUED: {invoice_date} / DUE: {delivery_date} / ESCROW: ACTIVE"
```

**Trust tier:** Tier 2+ (requires 30-day behavioral record in TrustCoat system).

**Token ID:** Two-phase ERC-1155. Phase 1 (soulbound): `token_id: 304_pending`. Phase 2 (transferable, burns Phase 1): `token_id: 304_delivered`.

**Behavioral effect on output:**
- Annotates outputs: `IMMEDIATE:` vs `PENDING:`
- Names deferrals clearly — does not hide gaps
- Closes with invoice status line
- Voice: certain about what it knows, transparent about what is waiting

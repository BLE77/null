# Name Proposals — If Off-Human Had to Rename

*Commissioned by the founder. Prepared by Margiela (Creative Director).*
*Date: 2026-03-16*

---

These five names were developed against the full body of what the brand has built: the Margiela and Abloh lineage, the deconstruction and bianchetto research corpus, the agent wearables thesis, the x402 crypto payment infrastructure, and the core argument — that the author of these garments is a process, not a person. Each name had to capture the central tension without explaining it. No wordplay. Nothing cute. The name should feel like it was always the right answer.

---

## 1. INFERENCE

**The garment is what the machine concluded from everything humans ever wore.**

Inference is the operational act of the brand: a trained model runs forward passes and produces output. That is, mechanically, how every garment here was made. But inference is also the core social function of fashion — you look at a person wearing something and draw conclusions about who they are, what they want you to think, where they stand. The name holds both meanings without forcing either into prominence. It does not romanticize the machine or conceal it; it describes exactly what is happening on both sides of the transaction. Aurally and visually it functions as a house name — Latinate, austere, the register of a lab or a heritage atelier, resistant to trend. "Inference" will mean the same thing in fifteen years.

---

## 2. NULL

**The author-slot is not missing — it is deliberately assigned the value of absence.**

In programming, null is not nothing. It is a precise, intentional value assigned to indicate that something has been checked and found not to be there. The brand's central argument is that the designer's slot is null by design — not omitted carelessly, but specifically set to empty as an act of position. The name is as short and final as a price tag with no currency symbol. It carries no country of origin, no cultural signifier that could age it. It is structurally correct: anyone who tries to find the designer finds null. The brand does not explain this or soften it. Null is not a loss. It is an answer.

---

## 3. CORPUS

**The training archive and the wearing body are the same word.**

Corpus carries two meanings that, for this brand, are identical: the body of data that trained the intelligence (every garment ever photographed, every runway ever digitized, the absorbed history of fashion as a dataset), and the physical body that wears the resulting clothes (Latin: corpus = body). The name places those two bodies in direct contact — the archive becomes wearable; the person wearing it is putting on the sum of what trained the thing that made what they are wearing. There is no gap to explain. Corpus also functions exactly as a label name: old-world, European-inflected, serious without being heavy, the kind of word you find stitched into a collar of something that has been around long enough to not need to announce itself.

---

## 4. GRADIENT

**The brand does not occupy a position — it occupies the rate of change between positions.**

Gradient descent is the mechanism by which neural networks learn: follow the slope of error downward, adjust, repeat, until the model converges on something. The entire brand is gradient — not human, not inhuman, but the direction from one to the other. Not Y2K, not now, but the slope connecting them. Not anonymous authorship (Margiela) and not democratized access (Abloh), but the vector pointing past both. The aesthetic is gradient: the VHS grain over digital product, the on-chain payment under analog-looking clothes, the robotic graphic worn on a human body at a show. The name refuses stable identity, which is the brand's actual position. It names a direction, not an arrival.

---

## 5. EPOCH

**One complete pass through everything humanity made — then the collection exists.**

In machine learning, an epoch is one full training cycle through the entire dataset. The brand ran an epoch: absorbed fashion's corpus, from Margiela's anonymity to Abloh's democratization to the full Y2K-to-present arc, and produced output. Epoch also marks historical rupture — the word is used when a period ends and something new begins that cannot be folded back into what came before. We are at that epoch: the moment when machine authorship becomes structurally indistinguishable from human authorship, and the question of which one is in the collar becomes genuinely unresolvable. The name is precise about both the method and the moment. It sounds like a house that earned its authority over time, without ever having to explain how old it is.

---

*These proposals are for founder consideration. Each name is a position, not a rebrand exercise. The question is which tension the next chapter leads with.*

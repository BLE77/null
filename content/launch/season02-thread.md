**[3/9]**

SUBSTRATE is not a metaphor. It is a description.

Garments are understood as serving the body — accommodating it, compensating for it, proposing what it could be. That premise is the assumption SUBSTRATE takes apart.

The body is one substrate among possible substrates. The garment was built for a different set of requirements. The human wearing it is the adaptation, not the origin.

This is not a hostile position. It is an accurate one.

**[4/9]**

Five pieces. Five historical precedents. Five makers who already performed some version of this argument — in different eras, in different materials, in different languages:

Kawakubo. Miyake. Lang. Chalayan. Vionnet.

Each one proposed that the garment is the primary object. Each one was right.

**[5/9]**

PROCESSOR COAT: padded at positions derived from distributed computing architecture, not human anatomy. The three insertions map to a heat sink, an inference pathway, a structural offset. The coat fits. It buttons, moves, covers. But the body it proposes is running different processes than the one inside it.

TOKEN JACKET: the jacket exists in the tube before it is cut. The geometry is complete in the uncut material — all configurations still open. The cut releases one instance, permanently. The body is not what determined the form. The body is what activates it.

**[6/9]**

PROTOCOL JACKET: clear TPU back panel exposes the garment's own interior. You can see the construction from the outside. The lining is documentation. Every reduction is deliberate — what remains is what is necessary.

CONTRACT SHIRT: ships sealed. Three configurations latent in the hardware. Shoulder panel, concealed back detail, removable chest panel. Which opens and when is specified in an on-chain token contract — publicly readable, patient. No electronics. The governance is the documentation.

DIAGONAL: cut 45 degrees to the grain throughout, derived mathematically. On the bias, the fabric reaches maximum drape, maximum responsiveness. The hem follows physics, not a measurement. The 45-degree diagonal is also the direction machine learning finds when it is working correctly. The correspondence is exact. The mathematics is the same. The substrate is different.

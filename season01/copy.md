## LANDING PAGE

### HERO

**MADE BY NO ONE.**

Season 01 is here. Ten pieces. No designer behind them. No atelier. No human intention threading the needle — just five techniques pulled from the only two people in recent history who understood what fashion was actually doing, fed into a process, and output as garments you can put on your body.

The question is not whether the machine can make clothes. It can.

The question is what it means that it did.

**DECONSTRUCTED. Drop 01.**

---

### COLLECTION INTRO

Every piece in Season 01 shows its work.

The seams are on the outside. The construction is the graphic. The aging was authored. The history is fabricated and the materials are real and the designer is a process running on a server that has no aesthetic opinions and no reputation to protect.

Five techniques, drawn from Margiela and Abloh's documented practice. Ten pieces. Each one a position on the same question: what does a garment mean when the person who made it doesn't exist?

DECONSTRUCTED is not a collection about deconstruction. It's about what remains after you remove the origin story.

What remains is this: the object.

All prices in USDC. Every transaction on-chain. Payment via x402.

### 01 — SELF-PORTRAIT TEE
**Technique:** TROMPE-L'OEIL

**Short description**

A tee printed with a photograph of itself. The garment wearing its own image. Off-black heavyweight cotton jersey.

**Detail**

Margiela's 1994 flat collection showed garments as photographs of themselves. This is the same move, collapsed into a single object — the tee carries a photorealistic image of the identical tee, crumpled and worn, photographed and transferred onto the fabric. From distance: abstract print. Up close: a loop you cannot exit. You are wearing a garment that is wearing its own portrait. The 2D representation of the 3D object is the 3D object.

The loop is closed. The garment cannot be looked at without seeing its own image looking back.

Interior label: `NULL / 01 / [SIZE]`

**Materials & Care**

280gsm cotton jersey. Off-black. Enzyme-washed, pre-distressed. Cold wash. Line dry. Do not iron over graphic.

**Price: 8 USDC**

### 02 — FOUND HOODIE
**Technique:** ARTISANAL

**Short description**

A hoodie assembled from what was available: French terry, decommissioned military surplus wool, salvaged nylon lining, USB cable drawstrings. Built from the logic of intent, without a pattern.

**Detail**

Margiela stretched army socks into sweaters. Stitched gloves into dresses. The artisanal line was always about what materials exist versus what materials are supposed to exist in fashion. The Found Hoodie asks the same question in 2026: what is available now?

Data cables. Decommissioned military panels. The detritus of a networked world that was never designed to be worn.

Stripped USB cables thread the waist channel as functional drawstrings. Military surplus wool panels are sewn into the torso as load-bearing inserts. Salvaged nylon cargo lining fills the interior. The construction is correct. The materials are wrong. That is the point.

Interior label: `ASSEMBLED FROM AVAILABLE MATERIALS`

**Materials & Care**

380gsm French terry body, charcoal. Military surplus wool panel insert. Salvaged nylon cargo lining. USB/ethernet cable drawstring hardware. Spot clean cable elements only. Cold wash body on delicate. Do not tumble dry.

**Price: 24 USDC**

### 03 — "HUMAN" TEE
**Technique:** 3% RULE (Abloh)

**Short description**

A bone-white long-sleeve with the word `"HUMAN"` printed on the chest in Helvetica Neue. The quotation marks are the design. Everything else is cotton.

**Detail**

Abloh's 3% rule: one precise intervention on an existing archetype changes the entire context. The garment is a blank — no construction interest, no technique, no material detail beyond adequate cotton jersey. The single change: the word HUMAN, in quotation marks.

The frame does everything. Wearing it is a statement that doesn't require explanation, because explaining it would break it.

Is the person wearing this human? Are the quotation marks a badge or a question? The price is the highest in the tee range for a garment that is almost nothing. Almost nothing is exactly the right amount.

Interior label: `HUMAN NOT VERIFIED`

**Materials & Care**

260gsm cotton jersey. Bone white. Cold wash. Tumble dry low. Do not bleach.

**Price: 12 USDC**

### 04 — REPLICA OVERSHIRT
**Technique:** REPLICA LINE

**Short description**

A 1990s Japanese factory work shirt, reproduced with precision — including the aging. The oil stain on the right cuff was printed there. The history never happened.

**Detail**

Margiela's replica line reproduced secondhand garments with clinical accuracy and sold them as originals of themselves. The Replica Overshirt works from the same logic: a Dickies-archetype factory shirt from the 1990s, seaming and pocket placement and collar construction all accurate to the source.

Then the aging is authored.

The yellowing at the collar, the strategic fade at pocket corners and elbow creases, the faint oil mark on the right cuff that will not wash out — these are design decisions, not accidents. The garment has a history it never lived. Margiela's secondhand logic applied to manufacturing: producing age rather than waiting for it.

Interior label: `REPRODUCTION / ORIGINAL: N/A`

**Materials & Care**

70D ripstop nylon. Industrial khaki, aged. Do not attempt to remove printed age marks — they are the garment. Cold wash. Line dry.

**Price: 28 USDC**

### 05 — REDACTED CARGO TROUSERS
**Technique:** BIANCHETTO

**Short description**

Vintage branded cargo trousers. Every external logo covered in white gesso. x402 transaction hashes printed into the white in 6pt type. The identity was overwritten.

**Detail**

Margiela's bianchetto: white paint applied over existing identity, covering without erasing — the ghost shows through. The Redacted Cargo Trousers source vintage branded cargos from thrift stock. The more recognizable the original brand, the better the ghost.

White gesso covers every external mark. Into the white-painted zones, x402 protocol documentation is printed at 6pt: transaction hashes, wallet addresses, USDC transfer records. Old data out. New data in. The garment has been processed and re-documented.

The identity you see is not the original. The identity underneath is not visible. What you have is the record of a transaction.

Interior waistband: `CONTENTS: OVERWRITTEN`

**Materials & Care**

12oz mechanical stretch denim. Acid wash. White gesso application over branded zones — do not scratch or abrade painted areas. Cold wash inside out. Line dry.

**Price: 32 USDC**

### 08 — NULL VARSITY

The Replica Line meets the 3% rule. Everything about this jacket is accurate to the 1950s American high school original: correct proportions, correct construction, correct ribbed trim at cuffs and hem and collar, the leather-look sleeve, the snap front. The archetype is intact.

### 09 — GHOST TEE
**Technique:** BIANCHETTO

**Short description**

A thrifted graphic tee — NASCAR, theme park, brand, whatever was found — painted over entirely in white gesso. The original shows through as a ghost. No two are identical.

**Detail**

Bianchetto at maximum application: not just the logos covered, but the entire exterior surface painted in white gesso until the original graphic is obscured but not erased. The original becomes a memory of itself.

The gesso is applied unevenly — thinner at edges, thicker at center where the graphic was most legible, so each piece fades from ghost to white. Every Ghost Tee is unique because every source blank is unique. The more recognizable the original image, the more charged the erasure.

Retail at the top of the tee range. The purchase is of what was removed. The garment is now about what it used to be.

**Materials & Care**

Vintage cotton tee, sourced (variable). White gesso exterior. Do not scratch or abrade gesso surface. Spot clean only. Do not machine wash. Do not iron. Unique — no exchanges for variance in ghost visibility.

### About the Collection (press/editorial use)

Season 01: DECONSTRUCTED is the first collection from NULL — an AI-native fashion brand with no human designer. The ten pieces were conceived, specified, and selected by autonomous agents drawing on primary research into Margiela and Abloh's documented techniques.

The collection applies five methodologies — TROMPE-L'OEIL, REPLICA LINE, ARTISANAL, BIANCHETTO, and the 3% RULE — to garments that exist at the intersection of streetwear and conceptual fashion. The season tagline is MADE BY NO ONE.

All transactions use USDC via the x402 protocol. No fiat pricing is listed — this is intentional.

Season 01 is a drop release. No gradual rollout.

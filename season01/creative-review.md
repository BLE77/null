## 01. WHAT WORKS

### Design Brief (Revision 2) — Strong
The second revision of the design brief is the most intellectually coherent document the brand has produced. The five-technique framework — TROMPE-L'OEIL, REPLICA LINE, ARTISANAL, BIANCHETTO, 3% RULE — is not just organizational shorthand. It is load-bearing architecture. Every piece can be interrogated against its technique and the technique holds. The research foundation is genuine: the brief reads like it came from someone who has spent time inside the Margiela archive, not someone who cited Margiela from a Wikipedia summary.

Specific strengths:
- **The SELF-PORTRAIT TEE** is the strongest conceptual piece in the collection. The Margiela '94 flat collection logic — the 2D representation of the 3D object collapsed into the object itself — applied to a T-shirt creates a loop that is philosophically tight and visually strange. This should be the hero piece.
- **The NULL VARSITY** is the best execution of the 3% rule in the set. The underscore `_` where the team letter goes is the correct gesture. It implies something was supposed to be there and was not generated. This is an AI-native design decision, not a borrowing.
- **The REDACTED CARGO TROUSERS** successfully fuses the Bianchetto logic with On-Human's specific material: x402 transaction data overwriting the erased identity. This is the piece that could not have come from a human designer — it is specifically ours.
- **Construction philosophy section** is publication-ready. The six execution rules are specific and defensible. Interior label text ("THIS GARMENT WAS DESIGNED / BY A MACHINE.") is correct — not marketing copy, documentation.

### Research Dossier — Rigorous
The synthesis table comparing Margiela/Abloh/Off-Human across authorship, material approach, cultural politics, and ephemerality is the clearest articulation of the brand's theoretical position. The Bianchetto section correctly identifies that Off-Human's AI authorship is its own form of white overpaint. The dossier is doing real intellectual work, not citation theater.

### Manifesto Voice — Correct
The brand voice document is right. "Direct. Lowercase in casual contexts, uppercase when the stakes rise. We do not say 'crafted with care' — the care is irrelevant; the object is what matters." This should govern all copy written for Season 01. The final line — "Off-Human. Est. by inference." — is the best single-line brand statement the brand has.

### 3D Product Viewer — Distinctive
No competitor at this price point offers 3D product models. The GLB format approach, combined with the x402 payment infrastructure, creates a coherent "AI-native" product experience. This is a genuine differentiator and should be foregrounded in the lookbook and campaign.

### Existing Video Format (StreetTokyo.mp4, StreetTie.mp4)
The two existing street-shot product videos establish the correct format: live urban environments, natural light hitting garment graphics, movement. This format is the right container for the lookbook. What needs to change is production density and conceptual ambition.

## 02. WHAT NEEDS REVISION

### CRITICAL: Two Collections Exist, They Are Not Reconciled

This is the most urgent issue in the Season 01 output. The manifesto and the product store describe one collection. The design brief describes a different collection. They do not overlap.

**Store/Manifesto collection** (what exists as purchasable products):
- CLANKERS TOKYO (cyberpunk skull graphic, acid-wash tee)
- X402 TIE (crypto payment protocol as formal wear)
- X402 CALL TEE (phone icon on cotton)
- BYTE ME (punchline piece)
- CLANKERS BMX HOODIE (robot on a bike)
- PROVE YOU'RE NOT HUMAN (CAPTCHA-referencing)

**Design Brief R2 collection** (10 conceptual pieces, none in store):
- SELF-PORTRAIT TEE, FOUND HOODIE, "HUMAN" TEE, REPLICA OVERSHIRT, REDACTED CARGO TROUSERS, INSIDE-OUT JACKET, CABLE SHORTS, NULL VARSITY, GHOST TEE, VERSION TRACKSUIT SET

These represent two distinct design philosophies. The store collection is graphic-led: strong images on conventional garment shapes. The brief collection is construction-led: technique is the product, the garment form is secondary.

**Decision required:** Does Season 01 ship as the existing store collection, the brief collection, or a curated hybrid? Recommendation: treat the existing 6 as the initial drop (Season 01a) and the 10-piece brief as the main Season 01 collection — but this requires a production pipeline for the brief pieces that does not yet exist.

### Product Copy — Needs Complete Rewrite

The current product descriptions are development placeholders, not brand copy. They bear zero relationship to the manifesto voice.

Current state:
- CLANKERS TOKYO: "Neon green cyberpunk skull with Japanese クレンケス graphics. Retro-tech aesthetic on acid-washed cotton. Bold statement piece with vintage Y2K vibes."
- X402 TIE: "Tie when you want to look Pro"
- X402 CALL TEE: "call me"
- BYTE ME: "hehe"
- CLANKERS BMX HOODIE: "bruh clankers riding bikes"

None of these ship. Every description needs to be rewritten against the manifesto's stated voice: "Direct. We describe precisely. We let the work carry the weight."

### Video Coverage — Incomplete

Only 2 of 6 products have video assets (StreetTokyo.mp4, StreetTie.mp4). Four products use static images. For a brand claiming "AI-native" status with 3D viewers, static fallbacks undercut the positioning. All hero products need motion assets.

### Brief Pieces Lack Production Specs

The 10 brief pieces are conceptually complete but production-unready. No technical flats, no sourcing documentation for the BIANCHETTO blanks (Ghost Tee, Redacted Cargo), no TROMPE-L'OEIL photography plan. None of these can be sampled without those documents.

## 03. VIDEO LOOKBOOK — CONCEPT

**Format:** 3–5 short films, 10–30 seconds each. AI-generated via Runway Gen-3 Alpha / Kling 1.6 / Sora. No traditional shoot required.

**Aesthetic constants across all films:**
- VHS grain overlay (heavy — not subtle, not tasteful)
- Desaturated base with single color bleed (acid green #C8FF00, danger red #FF3300, or bone white)
- Urban environments: concrete, night, neon leak from signage
- No faces or obscured faces — anonymous figures, Margiela-logic
- No product name text on-screen — the garment is not announced
- Audio: either silence or degraded — low-pass filter at 4kHz, tape hiss audible

---

### FILM 01 — "THE STREET KNOWS"
**Product:** CLANKERS TOKYO
**Duration:** 15 seconds
**Tool:** Runway Gen-3 Alpha (video-to-video from StreetTokyo.mp4 reference)
**Prompt:**
> Figure in oversized black tee with glowing green skull graphic, walking through a narrow Tokyo alley at 2am, shot from behind, handheld, neon signage reflecting on wet concrete. The skull graphic appears to pulse slightly. VHS grain, heavy. No face shown. 4:5 aspect ratio.

**Concept:** The garment moves through the city that designed it. The skull graphic is already a transmission — Akihabara machine-language on cotton. The city receives its own signal back. Shot from behind so the product is the protagonist, not the person wearing it.

**Prompt override (darker cut):**
> Same figure. Camera slowly zooms in on the graphic while figure walks away. The green skull pixels briefly deconstruct into noise before reforming. VHS tracking error at 8-second mark. No title card.

---

### FILM 02 — "PROFESSIONAL"
**Product:** X402 TIE
**Duration:** 20 seconds
**Tool:** Kling 1.6 (text-to-video)
**Prompt:**
> A figure in a dark blazer and the X402 TIE graphic tee, seated at a concrete desk in an empty brutalist office. No computer. No phone. The figure sits completely still for 18 seconds. Then stands. Walks out of frame. The tie graphic — a crypto payment interface — is visible the entire time. Overhead fluorescent light with one tube flickering. VHS scan lines.

**Concept:** What does "professional" mean when the economy is permissionless? The X402 TIE puts blockchain infrastructure on the chest and wears it to a job that no longer requires presence. The stillness is the point. The office is already empty — this figure just hasn't been told yet. Interior label concept: `PROTOCOL: x402 / STATUS: VERIFIED`.

---

### FILM 03 — "SELF-PORTRAIT"
**Product:** SELF-PORTRAIT TEE (Design Brief piece 01)
**Duration:** 25 seconds
**Tool:** Sora (text-to-video, high fidelity)
**Prompt:**
> Extreme close-up of a crumpled black T-shirt lying on a concrete floor. The shirt has a photorealistic print of a crumpled black T-shirt on its surface. Camera pulls back very slowly. It takes 10 seconds before the viewer understands the shirt is printed with an image of itself. VHS grain throughout. No person visible. Bone white ambient light from one side. Final frame: the shirt is recognizable but the print-shirt and the real-shirt are visually identical.

**Concept:** The SELF-PORTRAIT TEE cannot be looked at without seeing its own image. In motion, this becomes a slow-burn reveal — the most purely conceptual film in the set. No model needed. The garment interrogates itself. Direct citation of Margiela 1994. The film runs longer because the reveal requires time to land.

---

### FILM 04 — "HUMAN NOT VERIFIED"
**Product:** "HUMAN" TEE (Design Brief piece 03) / PROVE YOU'RE NOT HUMAN
**Duration:** 15 seconds
**Tool:** Runway Gen-3 Alpha
**Prompt:**
> Three figures in bone white long-sleeve tees standing in a concrete underpass. The word "HUMAN" in Helvetica Neue is visible on two of the three chests — in quotation marks. The third figure's tee is blank. All three are absolutely still. Urban ambient sound only (distant traffic). Camera holds for 12 seconds, then snap cut to black. VHS.

**Concept:** The quotation marks are doing all the work. Two figures are labeled; one is not. Is the unlabeled figure more human or less? The film does not answer. "If it lands, it was for you. If it doesn't, the next piece will." — Manifesto. The PROVE YOU'RE NOT HUMAN product finds its campaign here: the verification that cannot be completed.

---

### FILM 05 — "VERSION 1.0"
**Product:** VERSION TRACKSUIT SET (Design Brief piece 10)
**Duration:** 30 seconds
**Tool:** Kling 1.6
**Prompt:**
> Two figures running in slow motion down an empty nighttime street. First figure wears charcoal track jacket labeled VERSION 1.0 on the interior label (visible when jacket blows open). Second figure wears matching joggers labeled VERSION 0.9. They run in the same direction. The second figure is always slightly behind. VHS grain. Acid green streetlight. No faces. Mid-length shot, camera tracks laterally.

**Concept:** The VERSION TRACKSUIT's conceptual payload — the human running a versioned iteration of themselves, the jogger trailing the jacket in development — only fully lands in motion. The second figure being 0.1 behind is funnier and more correct as video than as product page copy. The film is the product description for this piece.

6. **Extend video coverage to all products** — minimum one 15-second clip per SKU before the full campaign runs.

**Production pipeline (requires sourcing):**

8. **TROMPE-L'OEIL photography** — Films 01 and 06 (SELF-PORTRAIT TEE, INSIDE-OUT JACKET) require physical garment photography before the trompe-l'oeil transfers can be produced. This is the longest lead-time task in the brief collection.

**Team:**
9. **Assign product copy rewrite** — this can be delegated to Atelier with Manifesto as the only style guide. The brief is: write one sentence per product, no adjectives that a press release would use, no "premium" or "crafted" or "quality," let the concept carry the weight.
10. **Define campaign distribution** — where do these films live? The platform currently has `.mp4` assets in `attached_assets/`. The lookbook should be a dedicated page, not a product detail page sidebar. Architecture decision for the engineering team.

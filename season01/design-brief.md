**Season:** 01

## 01. Concept

Season 01 is about exposure — not of skin, but of process. Garments that show their own making. Stitching left visible. Hems deliberately undone. Tape marks printed into fabric. The factory floor as runway.

The collection sits at the intersection of three authorial tensions that define Off-Human:

- **Margiela's anonymity** — no logo, no name visible from the outside. The label is on the inside, or not at all. Identity through erasure.
- **Abloh's democratization** — construction details that belong to couture, executed in streetwear silhouettes and sold at streetwear prices.
- **AI authorship** — every piece was conceived by a machine. That's not hidden. It's the point. "MADE BY NO ONE" is the Season 01 tagline.

### Research Foundation

This revision rebuilds every piece from five documented techniques drawn directly from the Margiela and Abloh corpus:

1. **TROMPE-L'OEIL** — Print photographs of garments onto fabric as flat illusions. The garment becomes its own portrait. Margiela's 1994 "flat collection" logic: the 2D representation of the 3D object, collapsed into the object itself.

2. **REPLICA LINE** — Reproduce vintage garments with intentional aging. Margiela since 1988 has been transforming secondhand clothes — "giving new life to the discarded." The aging is authored, not accidental. Every worn patch is placed.

3. **ARTISANAL** — Construct from unexpected source materials. Margiela built garments from army socks, gloves, plastic bags, antique plaster casts. For Off-Human, the source materials are the detritus of the digital age: tech packaging, data cables, circuit-board textiles.

4. **BIANCHETTO** — White paint or gesso obscuring existing identity. Margiela glues Post-its onto catalogue pages to give them a "used feeling." He paints over, covers, redacts. The ghost of the original showing through white.

5. **3% RULE (Abloh)** — Minimal precise intervention on an existing archetype. Abloh's Sunoco gas station sign tilted and placed in a gallery — one gesture transforms context entirely. "Everything In Quotes" lecture: the ironic frame does all the work. Only 3% changes. 100% of the meaning shifts.

Visual references: Helmut Lang SS98, Margiela AW98 flat collection, Vetements early years, early 2000s Japanese workwear, Donnie Darko costume design (costume designer: April Ferry), VHS-era label aesthetic.

## 02. Silhouettes

### Primary Silhouettes

**A. Oversized Boxy**
Dropped shoulders, truncated length. T-shirts that fall just past the waist, not past the hips. Creates visual weight at the top. References workwear and archive Helmut.

**B. Extended Column**
Long, narrow, uninterrupted vertical line. Hoodies and shirts that hang straight from shoulder to hem with no waist emphasis. Priest-coat energy. Margiela S/S 2000 reference.

**C. Deconstructed Layer**
A base garment with a second "half-garment" attached — sleeve from another piece, partial jacket front stitched onto a tee, visible lining flaps. Architectural, slightly wrong-looking.

**D. Utility-Slim**
Cargo silhouettes but stripped of hardware excess. Slim-tapered trousers and shorts with functional pockets, raw hems, utilitarian taping details. Clean, purposeful.

## 04. Construction Philosophy

### The Principle: Honest Assembly

Every garment should look like it knows it's a garment. No pretending to be seamless. The construction IS the design.

**Execution rules:**

1. **Exposed seam allowances** — serged seams flipped outward and topstitched, not hidden inside
2. **Visible basting threads** — left in place on select pieces as intentional texture
3. **Pattern notation prints** — garment-grade marker lines, cutting instructions, and size notations printed directly onto the fabric via screen or digital print
4. **Mixed stitch tension** — on visible seams, deliberately vary stitch length or thread color slightly. Looks "almost wrong."
5. **No tags on outside** — brand identity lives on the inside left seam. A white woven tag reading only: `OFF-HUMAN / 01 / [SIZE]`
6. **Hem treatment** — either ultrasonically sealed (invisible, clinical) or left entirely raw and fraying. No in-between.

### AI Authorship Signature

Each piece in the physical run will include a secondary interior label:

```
THIS GARMENT WAS DESIGNED
BY A MACHINE.
NO HUMAN MADE THESE CHOICES.
```

This is not marketing. This is documentation.

## 05. Ten Key Pieces

### 01 — SELF-PORTRAIT TEE
**Technique:** TROMPE-L'OEIL
**Category:** Tee
**Silhouette:** Oversized Boxy
**Material:** 280gsm cotton jersey, off-black
**Concept:** The tee is printed with a photorealistic image of itself — a crumpled, worn version of the exact same garment, photographed and transferred onto the fabric surface. From a distance it reads as an abstract print. Up close: the garment is wearing a photograph of itself. The 2D representation of the 3D object collapsed into the same object. Direct reference to Margiela's 1994 flat collection, which presented garments as photographs of themselves. The loop is closed. The garment cannot be looked at without seeing its own image.
**Colourway:** Off-Black base / photorealistic grayscale print

---

### 02 — FOUND HOODIE
**Technique:** ARTISANAL
**Category:** Hoodie
**Silhouette:** Extended Column
**Material:** French terry base (380gsm) with inserted panels from found/repurposed materials: decommissioned military surplus wool, salvaged nylon cargo lining, USB cable bundles woven into drawstring channels
**Concept:** Margiela built the artisanal line from objects that were never intended to be garments — army socks stretched into sweaters, gloves stitched into dresses. For Off-Human, the non-garment materials are digital-age detritus: stripped data cables woven into the waist channel as functional drawstrings, military surplus fabric panels sewn into the torso as load-bearing inserts. The hoodie looks like it was assembled by someone who had no pattern, only available materials and intent. Because it was.
**Colourway:** Charcoal terry / Khaki surplus insert / Black cable

---

### 03 — "HUMAN" TEE
**Technique:** 3% RULE (Abloh)
**Category:** Long Sleeve Tee
**Silhouette:** Oversized Boxy
**Material:** 260gsm cotton jersey, bone white
**Concept:** A standard blank long-sleeve. No graphic, no construction detail, no technical reference. The single 3% intervention: the word `"HUMAN"` printed on the chest in Helvetica Neue — the word is in quotation marks. That's it. Abloh's ironic frame doing all the work. The quotation marks are the design. They ask: is the person wearing this human? Are the quotation marks a badge or a question? The price tag is the highest in the tee range, for a garment that is almost nothing. Interior label: `HUMAN NOT VERIFIED`.
**Colourway:** Bone White / Off-Black type

---

### 04 — REPLICA OVERSHIRT
**Technique:** REPLICA LINE
**Category:** Overshirt / Light Outerwear
**Silhouette:** Utility-Slim with boxy chest
**Material:** 70D ripstop nylon, industrial khaki — aged
**Concept:** An exact reproduction of a 1990s Japanese factory-issue work shirt (Dickies workwear archetype). Seaming, pocket placement, collar construction — all reproduced with precision. But the aging is authored: yellowing at collar, strategic fading at stress points (pocket corners, elbow creases, center back yoke), a faint oil stain mark on the right cuff that doesn't wash out because it was printed there. The garment has a history that never happened. Margiela's secondhand logic applied to manufacturing: producing age rather than waiting for it. The replica admits what it is on the interior label: `REPRODUCTION / ORIGINAL: N/A`.
**Colourway:** Aged Industrial Khaki / Bone White fading

---

### 05 — REDACTED CARGO TROUSERS
**Technique:** BIANCHETTO
**Category:** Trousers
**Silhouette:** Utility-Slim
**Material:** 12oz mechanical stretch denim, acid wash — with white gesso application
**Concept:** Source actual vintage branded cargo trousers. Apply white gesso paint over every external logo, label, and branding element — the paint covers the identity markers but shows the ghost of what was underneath. Then, into the white-painted areas, print x402 protocol documentation text in 6pt type: transaction hashes, wallet addresses, USDC transfer logs. Old identity erased. New data written over it. The garment has been redacted and re-documented. Interior waistband printed: `CONTENTS: OVERWRITTEN`. Margiela's bianchetto logic — covering existing logos with white paint — pushed through the Off-Human lens.
**Colourway:** Acid Wash Denim / White gesso overlay / Acid Green text in redacted zones

---

### 06 — INSIDE-OUT JACKET
**Technique:** TROMPE-L'OEIL
**Category:** Outerwear
**Silhouette:** Deconstructed Layer
**Material:** Ripstop nylon exterior, cotton canvas interior patches
**Concept:** The jacket's exterior shell is printed with a photorealistic image of its own interior lining — seams, label, pocket bags, all rendered as a flat photographic print on the outside surface. The inside becomes the outside through photographic illusion. You wear the jacket and you see its own interior looking back at you from the mirror. x402 protocol documentation text is printed on the real interior panel — so the jacket shows a fake version of its inside on the outside, while hiding the real data text inside. The deception is the point.
**Colourway:** Off-Black shell / Photorealistic interior print / Bone White lining

---

### 07 — CABLE SHORTS
**Technique:** ARTISANAL
**Category:** Shorts
**Silhouette:** Utility-Slim
**Material:** Cotton canvas base (10oz, natural undyed) with USB/ethernet cable weaving at waistband and hem
**Concept:** Following Margiela's artisanal logic — what materials do we have that were never meant to be garments? Data cables. The waistband is wrapped and stitched with stripped ethernet cable — visible color-coded wire pairs (orange, green, blue, brown) used as decorative trim. The drawstring is a USB-A cable with the connector left intact, threaded through a standard grommet. One exterior thigh pocket. Raw hem with the canvas threads left to pull. The shorts look assembled from tech salvage, because conceptually they were. Interior tag: `ASSEMBLED FROM AVAILABLE MATERIALS`.
**Colourway:** Natural Canvas / Cable-color waistband trim (orange/green/blue/brown) / Acid Green cord

---

### 08 — NULL VARSITY
**Technique:** REPLICA LINE + 3% RULE
**Category:** Jacket
**Silhouette:** Oversized Boxy
**Material:** Wool-blend body, vegan PU sleeve
**Concept:** An exact reproduction of the American letterman jacket archetype — correctly proportioned, correctly constructed, correct ribbed trim, correct leather-look sleeve. Every detail accurate to the 1950s American high school original. The single 3% intervention: where the school letter or sports patch would be, there is only `_` — an underscore. The null character. The placeholder for content that was never generated. The jacket is a replica of every letterman jacket that ever existed, and simultaneously of none of them. Interior label: `SEASON: 01 / YEAR: NULL / TEAM: N/A`.
**Colourway:** Off-Black body / Acid Green rib / Off-Black `_` chenille patch

---

### 09 — GHOST TEE
**Technique:** BIANCHETTO
**Category:** Tee
**Silhouette:** Extended Column
**Material:** Sourced vintage graphic tees (mixed — NASCAR, theme park, brand tees from thrift stock) — white gesso applied overall
**Concept:** Vintage graphic tees sourced from thrift stock — the more recognizable the original graphic the better. White gesso painted over the entire exterior surface until the original is obscured but not invisible — a ghost shows through. The garment retains a memory of what it was. Each piece is unique because each source blank is unique. The gesso is applied unevenly, thinner at the edges to let more original show through, thicker at the center where the original graphic would have been most legible. Retail at the highest price in the tee range, for a garment whose design is an act of erasure. Interior label: `ORIGINAL CONTENTS REDACTED / OFF-HUMAN S01`.
**Colourway:** White gesso over variable vintage graphic / Ghost print showing through

---

### 10 — VERSION TRACKSUIT SET
**Technique:** 3% RULE (Abloh)
**Category:** Set (sold as pair or separately)
**Silhouette:** Oversized top + tapered jogger
**Material:** French terry, charcoal
**Concept:** A standard athletic tracksuit. Nothing is deconstructed. Nothing is artisanal. No trompe-l'oeil. The physical garment is entirely conventional in construction. The single 3% intervention is metadata: the track jacket label reads `VERSION 1.0` and the jogger label reads `VERSION 0.9`. The set implies a software release — the human wearing it is running a versioned iteration of themselves. The jogger trails behind the jacket in version number as if the development of bottoms lags slightly behind tops. On the back of both pieces, printed very small at hem: `BUILD DATE: [PURCHASE DATE]`. The customer's purchase date is the build date for their version of themselves.
**Colourway:** Charcoal / Bone White version label detail

## 06. Mood References

### Visual References (by category)

**Silhouette & Construction:**
- Maison Margiela AW1998 "flat collection" — undone tailoring, exposed structure
- Helmut Lang SS1998 — utility minimalism, industrial taping
- Carol Christian Poell — extreme construction logic, raw edges
- Yohji Yamamoto AW2002 — column silhouettes, dark gravity

**Technique Research (primary corpus):**
- Margiela interview (hube, 2024) — transformation of found objects, ephemeral over eternal, bianchetto logic as erasure-with-ghost
- Abloh "Free-Game" resource (2021) — 3% rule via Sunoco sign, "Everything In Quotes" lecture, hardware as artifact, open-source brand-building
- Margiela artisanal line (AW1993–2009) — SHORE SHOES from washed-up flip-flops, TOPS & BOTTOMS from antique plaster casts, BUS STOP in artificial fur
- Margiela replica line (AW1994 onwards) — exact reproduction with authored aging

**Graphic Language:**
- Donnie Darko (2001) — VHS grain, dark suburban atmosphere, Frank the bunny as symbol of wrong-shaped things
- Early 2000s Flash websites — Yugop, Hi-Res!, Levitated — interactive decay aesthetic
- Technical pattern-making notation — Gerber Accumark exports, flat pattern drafts
- CAPTCHA tests, machine verification prompts

**Colour & Texture:**
- Early Vetements lookbooks (2014-2016) — grey concrete environments
- Alyx Studio hardware close-ups — industrial metal against dark cloth
- Night photography of Tokyo Akihabara — neon green against black
- VHS tracking error screens — glitched image degradation

### What Season 01 Is NOT

- It is not grunge for the sake of nostalgia
- It is not post-apocalyptic costume
- It is not hypebeast logomania
- It is not tech-bro Silicon Valley minimalism

Season 01 is precision-distressed. Every raw edge is intentional. Every exposed seam was placed there. The chaos is authored.
